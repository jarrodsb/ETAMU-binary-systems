## Results

- Reinforcement Learning is a form of *unsupervised learning*. It works by executing a random action, recording the new state of the agent, applying a neural network to the state to obtain Action Probabilities, and from these probabilities obtain the action for the next step. This series of steps creates a trajectory for the agent.
- By taking a batch of trajectories, an RL program can calculate an overall sum of rewards (aka the "return") for each trajectory. It then applies the policy gradient training to itself, which enhances the probabilities for all actions resulting in a high-return trajectory. By always seeking for the highest reward, an RL program teaches itself how to complete a complicated task in the best way possible.
- The Alpha-Go program turned out to perform better from Reinforcement Learning training that was completely unsupervised. Ultimately through RL the program had learned moves in the game of Go that humans had never thought of, and in 2017 the computer beat the world's best human Go player.
