## Results

- I have read about 25% of the paper so far.
- The SPOCK dataset consists of 100,000 potential three-planet systems simulated each over 10^9 orbits of the inner planet, and make an 80/20 train/test split. This is stated to be computationally expensive.
- The training systems are generated near mean motion resonances (MMR), rather than uniformly sampling the phase space, because this is where stabilities most often occur.
